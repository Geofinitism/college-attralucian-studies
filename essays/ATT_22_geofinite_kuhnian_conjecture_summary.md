## Core trajectory

Thomas Kuhn's *Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (1962) dismantled the idea of scientific progress as linear accumulation and introduced paradigm shifts — but Kuhn's account remained sociological. He observed that paradigms are incommensurable (old and new frameworks cannot be fully translated into each other) but lacked a formal mechanism to explain why. The Geofinite-Kuhnian Conjecture bridges this gap: a Kuhnian paradigm is formally an Alphon — a finite, physically instantiated symbolic system with a measurable geometric curvature. Normal science is symbol manipulation within a stable Alphon. Anomalies are curvature stress — measurements that exceed the Alphon's resolution limit r_a or whose cost of representation ΔM becomes unsustainable. Scientific revolutions are Alphonic replacements: discarding one geometry for another. Incommensurability is not a puzzle but a theorem: there is no isomorphism between Alphons that preserves both volume and curvature. When paradigms shift, we are not merely changing our minds — we are physically migrating to a new geometric substrate.

## Key claim

**A Kuhnian paradigm is an Alphon. Incommensurability is a geometric theorem, not a philosophical puzzle.** There is no isomorphism between Alphons that preserves both volume and curvature — therefore, perfect translation between paradigms is geometrically impossible. Scientific progress is navigation through a curved manifold of possible symbol configurations; revolutions are the moments when curvature stress makes the current Alphon physically unsustainable.

## Key arguments and case studies

**The Ptolemaic case:** Epicycle-upon-epicycle accumulation is curvature stress made visible. Each new epicycle adds Nexils to the representation, spiking SGM and thermodynamic cost. The Copernican revolution was a shift to a lower-curvature Alphon where planetary motion could be represented with fewer Nexils. Incommensurability between Ptolemaic and Copernican frameworks is not a translation difficulty — it is a geometric impossibility.

**The 1971 decimalisation case:** The £sd mixed-radix Alphon (base-12 and base-20) was optimised for a specific human-scale geometry: 12 pence to a shilling meant halving, thirding, and quartering without remainders. The shift to base-10 was Alphonic Extinction. In base-10, a third of a pound is 33.33... pence — a recurring decimal. The "halfpenny rounding" rules were the scar tissue of two incommensurable geometries. Ghost Alphons persist: pre-decimal intuitions still influence how older generations calculate.

**The language geometry case:** "Tree" in English requires four containment spheres (t-r-e-e). 树 in Chinese requires one. The Pythagorean theorem in English and in Chinese are geometrically distinct objects — different provenance, different cost, different curvature — even though they point to the same empirical outcome. Mathematics is not language-independent; it is language-constituted.

**The base-14 / binary contrast:** 13 in base-14 is a Lone-Nexil Prime — one symbol, one sphere, minimal curvature. In binary, 13 is 1101 — four Nexils, four spheres, high SGM. These are not representations of the same object; in Geofinitism, they are geometrically distinct entities. "Alphonic Primes" are not eternal truths but geometric configurations that resist decomposition within a specific Alphon.
