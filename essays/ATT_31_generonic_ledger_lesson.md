### 4.1 Mercury's Excess Perihelion Precession

Classical Newtonian prediction: 531 arcseconds/century.  
Observed total precession: 574.1 arcseconds/century.  
**Excess: 43.1 arcseconds/century** — standard GR attribute this to spacetime curvature.

$$k = 1.67 \times 10^{21} \text{ kg·s}^2/\text{m}$$

The FM formula recovers 43.1 arcseconds/century — **exactly** — without invoking spacetime curvature.

The constant k is the **local cost density** — the rate of Generonic expenditure per unit acceleration at solar-system scales.

### 4.2 Hydrogen Electron Stability

Classical electrodynamics predicts that a circular orbit radiates energy and spirals inward to collapse in ~10⁻¹¹ seconds. The hydrogen atom exists — this is the classical instability problem.

No wavefunction, no collapse, no radiation: the orbit is genuinely stable.

Spiral galaxies maintain approximately flat rotation curves (v ≈ const.) at large radii, where Newtonian dynamics predicts v ∝ 1/√r. The conventional explanation posits a dark matter halo.
