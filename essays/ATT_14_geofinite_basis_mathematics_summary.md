## Core trajectory

A four-postulate derivation of arithmetic from physical measurement alone. The historical introduction identifies the shared blind spot of all existing foundations programmes (Platonism, logicism, strict finitism): they all treat symbols as zero-volume abstract marks. Once symbols are required to occupy positive, finite, measurable volume — to "pay rent in actual space" — arithmetic is rederived from scratch. Addition is physical merging of Nexil configurations followed by relaxation via carry rules to the unique densest stable state. The abacus is not a model of arithmetic: it is arithmetic. The essay closes with three explicit empirical claims that could in principle falsify the entire framework, and invites contestation by experiment rather than by philosophy.

## Key claim

Arithmetic is a physical science: **2 + 2 = 4 is not a Platonic truth, a logical truth, or an analytic truth — it is the report of a physical relaxation process inside a finite container with indivisible unit symbols.** The laws of logic are the observed laws of geometric movement for distinguishable symbols; carry rules are discovered, not invented; and every computing device from abacus to GPU is a fast, miniaturised instance of the same process.

## The Four Minimal Postulates

| Postulate | Statement |
|-----------|----------|
| **1 — Physicality** | Every symbol that actually exists is a physical configuration of matter-energy |
| **2 — Finite Minimum Volume** | There exists a smallest length scale ℓ₀ at which differences in physical configuration can be reliably distinguished and communicated between observers |
| **3 — Closure** | The observable universe is finite; its information capacity is bounded above by ≈10¹²⁰–10¹²³ bits (Bekenstein-Bousso covariant entropy bound) |
| **4 — Distinguishability** | Two symbols are distinct if and only if at least one elementary volume unit v₀ differs in state between them |

These four are not philosophical stipulations — they are direct reports of existing measurement, each falsifiable by experiment.

## Key evidence and arguments

- **The shared blind spot**: All three historical camps (Platonism, logicism, strict finitism) share the unspoken assumption that symbols have no intrinsic volume and no intrinsic geometry. This is empirically false — every symbol ever inscribed has occupied positive, measurable volume. Removing this assumption is the entire essay.
- **Density Addition theorem**: Uniqueness follows from the three constraints (N_max, distinguishability, minimal unused capacity). No alternative stable configuration exists without violating the Alphonic Limit or Distinguishability postulate. The example: merging two copies of "2" forces a carry chain that terminates in "4" — no other stable terminal state is possible.
- **The abacus argument**: The abacus is a macroscopic-scale direct instantiation of the Geofinite system: beads = Nexils; rods and frame = Alphonic Space; merging bead configurations = addition; carry rule = density-overflow management. This is not an analogy. Every computing system shares this structure.
- **Physical origin of carry rules**: Standard arithmetic and logic are "the paths of least resistance for information processing in a finite geometric universe." Alternative carry rules are physically possible but less efficient — more energy, more time, more complex mechanisms. Logic is discovered, not invented.
- **Falsifiability**: The three empirical claims are explicitly stated and explicitly open to experimental contest. "If cosmology or quantum gravity ever demonstrates an actually infinite information capacity in a finite spatial volume, Claim 2 fails and classical mathematics regains its footing." This is unusually direct falsifiability for a foundations programme.
- **Positioning against alternatives**: Against strict finitism — gives the *physical reason* why proof heights must be restricted, not just the restriction. Against Deutsch/Tegmark — derives mathematics *from* the physical carrier, not physics *from* mathematics. Against category/type theory — "every morphism must pay a nexil toll."
