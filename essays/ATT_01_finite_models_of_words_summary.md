## Core trajectory

This essay traces the evolution of word models from dimensionless tokens through geometric hyperspheres and phonetic manifolds to the transducer framework — showing how grounding words in finite measurement transforms linguistics from abstract symbol manipulation into an empirically testable, dynamical science.

## Key claim

A word is a finite transducer that compresses measurements — both internal (distances within semantic space) and external (real-world sensor readings) — into structured corpus form, making meaning a measurable interaction rather than an abstract reference.
