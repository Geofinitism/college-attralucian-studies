## Core trajectory

The Ket Limit identifies the precise epistemic boundary where the infinite-dimensional Hilbert space formalism of quantum mechanics crosses from empirical description into Platonic projection — and Geofinitism rebuilds quantum mechanics within that boundary: redefining the quantum state as an attractor manifold reconstructed from finite Alphon measurements, replacing the Gaussian with the Circular Uncertainty Distribution, grounding the exponential in finite combinatorics, and dissolving singularities through finite geometry.

## Key claim

The Geofinitist Resolution Bound ε_total ≥ max(U_ex, 1/b, L/N^(1/d_P)) defines the hard epistemic limit of all quantum description; beyond it, |ψ⟩ is not physics but Platonic projection; rebuilding within this bound produces **Finite Quantum Mechanics**, which naturally eliminates singularities and provides a path to finite quantum gravity.

## Key evidence and arguments

- **Wavefunction collapse as artefact**: "quantum weirdness" is an artefact of over-extending the formalism beyond the Ket Limit, not a physical phenomenon requiring metaphysical explanation
- **Superposition as geometry**: not a physical reality but a geometric property of the reconstructed manifold — a coordinate representing maximal uncertainty or instability between basins of attraction
- **Exponential from combinatorics**: the Boltzmann-form distribution p_j ≈ (1/Z)e^{−βε_j} ± Δp_j emerges from counting finite arrangements; the infinite-precision exponential is the Δp_j → 0 limit — a useful fiction
- **Hydrogen atom**: the stable orbital is a closed geodesic on the reconstructed attractor manifold; stability is geometric inevitability, not a quantised law
- **Pauli exclusion as approximate**: Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions reformulated with measurable numbers; Pauli exclusion becomes approximate; Bose-Einstein condensation becomes a smooth crossover
- **Quantum gravity**: spacetime is a finite symbolic manifold with the Planck scale as fundamental alphabet; "infinite density" singularities (black holes, Big Bang) replaced by finite regions of size ~ℓ_P
