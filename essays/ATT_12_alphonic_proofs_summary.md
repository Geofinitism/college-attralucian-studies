## The Five Proofs

### Proof 1 — No Invariant Representation (Analytic)

**Theorem**: No bijective, volume-preserving, curvature-invariant map exists between Measured Numbers in different Alphons.

**Key step**: For SGM equality, A₁k₁ = A₂k₂ is required. Substituting k_i ≈ log_{A_i}(M) gives A_i/ln(A_i) equal for both Alphons. But g(A) = A/ln(A) is **monotonically increasing for A > e**, so A₁ ≠ A₂ ⟹ g(A₁) ≠ g(A₂). Contradiction.

**Consequence**: Every base conversion must change total volume or geometric curvature or both.

### Proof 2 — The Lone-Nexil Prime (Elementary Arithmetic)

**Theorem**: A prime p occupying one containment sphere in its native Alphon occupies many in other Alphons. Geometric identity is Alphon-dependent.

**Key case**: In base-(p+1), any prime p > A is a single Nexil — one sphere. In base-10, the same prime requires ⌊log₁₀(p)⌋ Nexils. For p = 8191: 1 sphere vs. ∼4 spheres. Since geometry is identity, these are different objects.

**Deepest cut**: Primality — arguably the most fundamental invariant in number theory — is not representation-invariant. A prime is a geometric configuration, and its geometry changes with the Alphon.

### Proof 3 — The Attralucian Nyquist Theorem (Computational / Spectral)

**Theorem**: Representing low-curvature meaning in high-curvature substrates requires oversampling proportional to the Alphon size ratio. Binary is the worst possible substrate.

**Nyquist bound for symbols**: N_substrate ≥ 2·log_B(A) in the information-theoretic limit; volumetrically, cost is cubic in the logarithm (because containment is 3D and uncertainty isotropic).

**Sonification argument**: Map each Nexil value to a pure frequency; generate waveforms for the same magnitude in binary, decimal, base-100. Binary produces harsh two-tone beating; decimal produces a rich harmonic series; base-100 approaches a smooth continuous sweep. The same magnitude sounds completely different — **audible proof** that representational geometry changes with the Alphon.

**Spectral reading**: Binary has narrow bandwidth, 2 components, high spectral density. Base-100 has wide bandwidth, 100 components, approaching a continuum. Meaning-curvature manifests as spectral curvature.

**Named theorem note**: The "Attralucian Nyquist Theorem" is the first theorem in the series to bear the College name as part of its title.

### Proof 4 — Takens Inequivalence of π (Nonlinear Dynamics)

**Theorem**: The digit sequences of π in different Alphons produce geometrically inequivalent attractors under Takens delay embedding. Not because they encode π differently — but because they *are* different geometric sequences.

**Background**: Kevin Haylett's prior empirical work showed that π's digits are statistically random under classical tests but geometrically spectacular under Takens 3D delay embedding, with structure strongly dependent on τ. Vision-language models (CLIP) can distinguish π from random sequences purely from the visual geometry of the attractor.

**Alphonic extension**: If π were base-invariant, its digit sequences in different Alphons should produce equivalent attractors (up to diffeomorphism). They do not.

**Predicted attractor geometries**:

| Alphon | Attractor | SGM | Curvature |
|--------|----------|-----|-----------|
| Binary | Fractal filamentary coil | Highest | Deepest |
| Decimal | Coiled-to-scaffolded, visible structure | Medium | Medium |
| Base-100 | Crystalline lattice, flat, regular | Lowest | Shallowest |

**Key insight**: The delay parameter τ in Takens embedding does exactly what changing the Alphon size does — it modulates the curvature of the symbolic manifold.

**Conclusion**: π-in-binary and π-in-base-100 are different geometric sequences with different attractor topologies. They are not diffeomorphic. They are different manifolds.

### Proof 5 — Alphonic Prime Collisions (Advanced Arithmetic)

**Theorem**: In odd bases A ≥ 3, distinct primes can be Alphon-equivalent. Primality is not invariant under representation.

**Corollary (Uniqueness of Binary)**: Base 2 is the only Alphon in which every number has a unique digit expansion with no positional ambiguity. This makes binary appear fundamental — but this is constraint, not truth. Higher Alphons have richer structure and lower curvature.

## Key evidence and arguments

- **The decisive analytic move**: g(A) = A/ln(A) is monotonically increasing for A > e. This single fact makes the No Invariant Representation Theorem a rigorous proof rather than a demonstration.
- **Lone-Nexil Prime as the simplest case**: 1 sphere vs. 4 spheres for p = 8191. The argument is elementary school arithmetic plus the geometric identity principle. The simplicity is the point — it requires no sophisticated machinery.
- **Sonification of base conversion**: The audible difference between binary, decimal, and base-100 representations of the same magnitude is genuinely observable and constitutes a physical demonstration, not a metaphor. The same event sounds different in different Alphons.
- **Takens attractor inequivalence is visual**: Side-by-side rendering of the binary and base-100 attractors of π produces visibly non-diffeomorphic manifolds. This is direct empirical evidence accessible without mathematical training.
- **Riemann Hypothesis framing is clearly speculative**: The text explicitly says "This is not a proof in the classical sense (which requires assuming the continuum). But it is a dissolution: the RH is not a conjecture about abstract complex functions — it is a statement about the geometry of finite representational growth."
- **Binary Tyranny diagnosis**: The entire computational civilisation is built on the least efficient Alphon, for historically contingent engineering reasons (transistor on/off simplicity). The von Neumann bottleneck, memory wall, and energy cost of computation are identified as symptoms of Alphonic mismatch.
- **Quantum gravity crisis**: At Planck scale, r_α ≈ ℓ_Pl, so V_α ≈ ℓ_Pl³ ≈ 10⁻¹⁰⁵ m³. Representing even 10 qubits (1024 states) would require containment volumes larger than the observable universe. Continuum mathematics becomes physically impossible exactly where quantum gravity needs it most.
