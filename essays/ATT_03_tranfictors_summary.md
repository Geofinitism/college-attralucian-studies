## Core trajectory

This essay synthesises the Useful Fictions model and the Transducers model from Essay 01 into a single unified framework — the Tranfictor — and introduces a measurable fiction quality metric that makes semantic precision quantifiable and falsifiable, bringing linguistic theory within the scope of Finite Mechanics.

## Key claim

Words function as tranfictors: compressed transducers of meaning whose semantic precision can be measured by a fiction quality metric (Quality ≈ 1 − Compression Ratio), where compression ratio reflects the number of possible referents a word maps to in a corpus. This metric makes the study of language falsifiable and cross-disciplinary.

## Key evidence

- **Fiction quality examples:** "cheetah" ~99% (one biological referent, low compression); "chair" ~50% (100+ referents — stools, thrones, office chairs); "freedom" ~20% (near-infinite interpretations, high compression)
- **Quality = 1 − Compression Ratio** — a quantifiable, testable formula grounded in corpus statistics
- **Reader as active co-author:** "chair" in a meeting room decompresses to "ergonomic office chair"; in a dining context to "wooden dining chair" — the reader's context acts as a high-pass filter
- **SUA as falsifiability tool:** the complexity of a word's SUA directly correlates with its compression level and low fiction quality
- **LLM implication:** models should embed high-quality terms (e.g., "quantum superposition") more stably than low-quality terms (e.g., "fairness," "consciousness")
