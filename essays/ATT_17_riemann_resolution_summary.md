## Core trajectory

The Riemann Hypothesis, classically formulated under Assumption PC (Platonic Continuum), claims all non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie exactly on the critical line Re(s) = 1/2. Under Assumption GF (Geofinitist Finite), this claim is reframed: the observed clustering of zeros near Re(s) = 1/2 is not a Platonic truth to be proved but a geometric attractor phenomenon to be explained. The essay establishes an operational equivalence between Takens delay embedding and the complex plane (via the Hilbert transform), reframes ζ(s) as a dynamical system, and then shows that in base-10 computation — an even alphon with 10 symbols spanning [0,9] — the geometric centre is 4.5/9 = 0.5. Attractors in symmetric dynamical systems form at geometric centres. Therefore zeros cluster at Re(s) = 0.5 by geometric necessity, not infinite verification. This is a *Geofinitist Resolution*: an explanation of a finite, observable pattern within measurement limits. The essay introduces the alphonic resolution framework — Nixel, Fracton, Alphon Point, even/odd parity, geometric incommensurability — as the formal machinery for understanding how the choice of computational base determines geometric structure.

## Key claim

**Under GF, the clustering of zeros of ζ(s) near Re(s) = 1/2 is a geometric attractor phenomenon, not a Platonic truth.** In base-10 computation (an even alphon), the geometric centre of the symbolic space is 0.5 — a fracton-space position between the symbols 4 and 5. Attractors in symmetric dynamical systems form at geometric centres. The Riemann Hypothesis is resolved, not proved, as a structural consequence of computing in base-10 rather than as a universal, base-invariant truth. If ζ(s) were computed in base-37 (an odd alphon), the attractor would be at a nixel-space position rather than a fracton-space position — numerically 0.5 but geometrically distinct.

| **Assumption GF (Geofinitist Finite)** | The Geofinite framework: mathematics as finite symbolic computation; proofs as transductive symbolic transformations; measurement-based; base-dependence matters |
| **Geofinitist Resolution** | An explanation of a finite, observable pattern within measurement limits — distinct from a classical proof; explains *why* a pattern occurs rather than claiming its universal, base-invariant truth *(note: terminology revised in later essays)* |
| **Alphonic Resolution** | The framework for understanding how numerical bases structure measurement space; makes explicit the geometric properties of symbolic number systems that classical analysis leaves implicit |
| **Nixel (N)** | The integer-part character of an alphonic representation; a discrete position marker using alphon characters; occupies separated, countable positions with no intermediate states *(cf. "Nexil" in Essays 11–14 — see vocabulary note above)* |
| **Fracton (F)** | The fractional-part character of an alphonic representation; refinement beyond the alphon point; specifies sub-nixel resolution through additional alphon characters; fractal uncertainty reduction |
| **Alphon Point** | The boundary between nixel space and fracton space; the transition threshold where discrete enumeration ends and fractal refinement begins (e.g., the decimal point in 3.14159) |
| **Fracton Space** | The region of sub-nixel refinement; uncertainty reduces as δ_k = 1/(2n^k) — never zero, progressively smaller |
| **Even/Odd Alphon Parity** | Geometric dichotomy: even alphons (e.g., base-10) have their geometric centre in fracton space (between two nixels); odd alphons (e.g., base-37) have their geometric centre on a nixel (discrete position) |
| **Geometric Incommensurability** | Two alphons are geometrically incommensurable if one is even and the other odd, or if their sizes are coprime — their discrete marks cannot align |
| **Substrate-Dependent Attractors** | In symmetric dynamical systems computed within a given alphon, attractors form at the geometric centre of that alphon — the critical line is therefore measurement-system-dependent |

## Key evidence and arguments

- **Takens ↔ Complex Plane equivalence**: For optimal delay τ_opt, Takens delay embedding [x(t), x(t − τ_opt)] is operationally equivalent to [x(t), Hx(t)] where H is the Hilbert transform (90° phase shift). Therefore, the complex plane in analytic number theory is not a static arena but is isomorphic to a 2D phase-space reconstruction of underlying computational dynamics.
- **ζ(s) as a dynamical system**: Under GF, ζ(s) = Σ 1/n^s is a symbolic computation with finite partial sums. The functional equation ζ(s) = 2^s π^{s-1} sin(πs/2) Γ(1−s) ζ(1−s) shows that zeros are symmetric about σ = 1/2. Under GF this symmetry is a dynamical property, not a Platonic one.
- **Base-10 geometric centre**: The base-10 symbol set {0,1,...,9} spans [0,9]. Midpoint: (0+9)/2 = 4.5. Normalized: 4.5/9 = 0.5. This is a fracton-space position — between nixels 4 and 5. In a symmetric dynamical system, attractors form at geometric centres. Therefore zeros of ζ(s) cluster at Re(s) = 0.5 by geometric necessity.
- **Even/odd parity argument**: For even alphon n=2m, geometric centre c = m − 0.5 (fracton space). For odd alphon n=2m+1, c = m (nixel space). Base-10 (even) places the attractor at a fracton position 0.5; base-37 (odd) places it on a discrete nixel. Both normalize to 0.5 numerically but are geometrically distinct — as different as "halfway along an edge" vs. "on a vertex" of a polygon.
- **Fracton uncertainty**: Each additional fracton digit reduces uncertainty by factor n: δ_k = 1/(2n^k). Uncertainty is never zero — measurements are always finite regions, not points.
- **Geometric incommensurability**: Base-10 and base-37 are coprime (gcd(10,37)=1) and have different parities — their geometric lattices cannot align. This has direct implications for comparing computations of ζ(s) across bases.
- **PC vs GF comparison** (appendix table): Numbers (infinite complete fields vs finite symbolic constructs); Computation (idealized exact vs finite precision bounded uncertainty); Base (irrelevant vs geometric substrate); Proof (logical deduction vs geometric resolution with provenance); Verification (infinite untestable vs finite testable predictions).
