## Core trajectory

The Fermi Paradox is conventionally framed as a problem of existence (where are they?) or distance (they can't reach us). This essay proposes it is fundamentally a problem of resolution: a failure of Alphonic measurement. Advanced civilisations, driven by thermodynamic efficiency, inevitably migrate to higher Alphons — larger alphabets, lower representational curvature, less energy per unit of information transmitted. Our binary/decimal detection apparatus constitutes catastrophic undersampling of any such signal, exactly as described by the Attralucian Nyquist Theorem. The π-geometry experiments (Essay 13) provide direct empirical proof-of-concept: structured information can pass every statistical randomness test while remaining geometrically visible to trajectory-based analysis. The Fermi Paradox is not resolved by finding aliens — it is dissolved by recognising we have been measuring with the wrong geometry all along. The solution is a Geofinite SETI Protocol: geometric receivers based on Takens reconstruction, vision-language model analysis, and TBT architecture.

## Key claim

Advanced civilisations broadcast in Higher Alphons that optimise thermodynamic efficiency. To binary/decimal receivers, these transmissions are **geometrically indistinguishable from thermal noise** — not because they are weak, but because the Attralucian Nyquist Barrier causes geometric collapse of high-Alphon structure into apparent randomness. **We are not alone; we are simply Alphon-blind.**

## Key evidence and arguments

- **Thermodynamic inevitability of Higher Alphons**: Advanced civilisations face the trade-off between binary (low ΔM per symbol but high k) and higher bases (high ΔM per symbol but low k). The optimal point is substrate- and energy-dependent, but always above binary for information-dense communication. They would view binary transmissions as we view Morse code — technically functional but wastefully verbose.
- **The SGM comparison table** (reproduced from Essay 11): binary requires 40 containment spheres for M≈10¹², base-100 requires only 6 — a 6.7:1 volume ratio for the same information. The thermodynamic consequence is direct.
- **Attralucian Nyquist Barrier applied to SETI**: A base-100 symbol requires at minimum log₂(100) ≈ 6.64 binary bits (information), but geometric preservation requires (6.64)³ ≈ 293 binary containment volumes. A binary receiver detecting a base-100 transmission is not undersampling slightly — it is undersampling by a factor of ~44 volumetrically.
- **Spectral collapse**: A base-100 transmission occupies ∼99 frequency components (wide bandwidth, smooth sweep). Binary receivers are designed for 1 frequency component (narrow bandwidth, high-contrast pulses). The 100-state signal collapses spectrally into what is indistinguishable from thermal noise — not because it is weak, but because it is geometrically oversampled into noise.
- **The π precedent as proof-of-concept**: The π digit sequence passes AMI, transition matrix, RQA, PCA, and KS tests — statistically indistinguishable from noise. Yet Takens 3D delay embedding reveals clearly distinct topology (coil at τ=1, scaffold at τ=5), measurable via vision-language model embeddings. This demonstrates at human scale that the scenario is real and detectable with the right instruments.
- **The four-part Signature Profile** as a positive detection criterion: signals from higher-Alphon civilisations should pass randomness tests AND exhibit low-curvature topology under Takens AND cluster in vision-model space AND show τ-invariant topology. This is a falsifiable, operationalisable research programme.
- **Existing SETI archives as immediate target**: The data may already exist. Reanalysing existing radio telescope archives with Takens + vision-model analysis is proposed as the immediate step.
