## The Myth of Base Invariance

Conventional mathematics assumes:

$$N_{(b_1)} = N_{(b_2)} = N$$

— the same number exists identically across every base b. This presumes an immaterial realm where symbols are infinitely compressible and transformations are lossless. In a finite, measurable universe, both assumptions are false. Every mark, pixel, or byte occupies finite space and requires finite work to inscribe.

The myth of base invariance survives only by ignoring the geometry of representation.

## The Alphonic Framework: Nexils and the Alphonic Limit

An **Alphon** A is a finite set of distinguishable symbols used to encode measurable magnitudes:

$$\mathcal{A} = \{\mathrm{Nexil}_1, \mathrm{Nexil}_2, \ldots, \mathrm{Nexil}_A\}$$

Each Alphon has measurable properties: alphabet size A, per-Nexil volume V_nex, substrate S, and intrinsic resolution Δr.

A **Nexil** n_i is a single finite symbol within an Alphon — not a static mark but an **event of distinction**. Each Nexil carries:

$$n_i = \{\text{form},\; V_{\mathrm{nex}},\; \text{provenance},\; \Delta M\}$$

where ΔM is its **Meaning Flux** — the measurable work of representing information within the substrate. Meaning is not a static property; it is dynamic, context-dependent, and carries temporal provenance.

### Why the Alphonic Limit Must Be Spherical

There exists a minimal volume within which a single Nexil can be uniquely realised and later retrieved — the **Alphonic Limit** V_α. Three constraints converge to make this volume spherical:

1. **No measurable edge** — any attempt to measure the boundary of a region smaller than V_α requires instruments whose uncertainty already exceeds that boundary. A shape defined by immeasurable edges has no defined edges.
2. **No preferred orientation** — orientation cannot be resolved within the Alphonic Limit; no axis may be privileged. Only a geometry in which all directions are equivalent satisfies this.
3. **Minimal surface for a given volume** — the sphere minimises surface area, minimising measurement noise or distortion at the boundary.

From these constraints:

$$\boxed{V_{\alpha} = \frac{4}{3}\pi r_{\alpha}^{3}}$$

This sphere is not a Platonic ideal. It is a **statistical envelope of uncertainty** — the region inside which the physical realisation of a Nexil may vary without losing identity.

Key consequences:
- **Containment, not occupation**: the Nexil is contained *by* the sphere, not identical to it
- **Isotropy of meaning**: each Nexil radiates meaning equally in all directions within its containment sphere
- **Additivity without overlap**: distinct Nexils occupy tangent or adjacent spheres
- **Curvature as density**: denser packing of containment spheres → greater curvature; larger Alphons produce flatter representational curvature
- **π as emergent**: π appears in V_α not as a universal constant but as the consequence of isotropic uncertainty in three dimensions; in other manifolds, π_A may differ slightly

## The Spherical Symbolic Geometry Mean (SGM)

Different Alphons representing the same physical quantity use different numbers of Nexils with different packing densities. This produces different representational curvatures. The **SGM** captures this:

$$\boxed{\text{SGM}_{\mathcal{A}}(k) = \left(\frac{3Ak}{4\pi r_{\mathcal{A}}^{3}}\right)^{1/3}}$$

This is the effective radius of a single sphere that would contain the entire symbolic density of k Nexils from Alphon A, were their containment volumes fused into one unit.

| SGM regime | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| High SGM | Flat, distributed meaning — large A, shallow k |
| Low SGM | Curved, compressed meaning — small A, deep k |
| SGM ≈ r_α | Operating at the edge of distinguishability |
| SGM ≪ r_α | Physically impossible — containment spheres collapse |

### SGM in the Quantum Regime

At r_α = 0.1 nm (quantum-confined substrate), encoding M ≈ 10¹² gives:

| Alphon | A | k | SGM (nm) |
|--------|---|---|---------|
| Binary | 2 | 40 | 0.134 |
| Quaternary | 4 | 20 | 0.121 |

At this scale, each Nexil costs ΔM ≥ 18 eV to localise. All values are within one order of r_α — we are at the edge of measurement. **Binary is fragile. Base-100 is robust. There is no neutral ground.**

## The No Invariant Representation Theorem

This is the formal resolution of the essay's central question.

**Theorem:** If A₁ ≠ A₂, then no bijective, volume-preserving, curvature-invariant map exists between the Measured Numbers of A₁ and A₂.

**Proof:** Assume such a map f exists. Then:

$$\text{SGM}_{\mathcal{A}_1}(k_1) = \text{SGM}_{\mathcal{A}_2}(k_2), \quad V_N^{(1)} = V_N^{(2)}$$

But from the SGM table: SGM_binary(40) = 0.134 ≠ 0.110 = SGM_hex(10). And V_α is fixed by r_α — no compensation is possible. Contradiction. ∎

Therefore:
- Every Alphon defines its own finite container geometry
- Each Nexil carries provenance and is contained within a measurable volume
- Conversion between Alphons changes representational density and curvature
- "Base" is a historical convenience; the Alphon is the measurable manifold of representation

Formally: A₁ ≠ A₂ ⟹ no invariant transformation of Measured Numbers.

## The Cost of Distinction

The Alphonic Limit is not a technological artefact. It is the **epistemic horizon of measurement itself**. As r_α shrinks toward the quantum frontier (∼0.1 nm, the scale of atomic orbitals), containment spheres begin to interfere — a '7' becomes epistemically indistinguishable from an '8'. The Nexil ceases to be uniquely realised.

This reveals the **Cost of Distinction**:

$$\Delta M = f(A, S, r_{\alpha})$$

The relational work required to maintain the mutual distinguishability of all A Nexils within an Alphon on substrate S at Alphonic radius r_α.

- As A increases: ΔM increases — distinguishing 100 symbols requires more work than distinguishing 2
- As r_α decreases: ΔM increases — denser packing raises the cost of preventing uncertainty envelope overlap

This is the true, non-Platonic boundary condition on mathematics.

## Representation is Alphonic

Every physical law is inscribed in some Alphon. When we write F = ma, we are writing a curved sentence in the Alphon of real-valued decimal notation. Change the Alphon — use binary, Roman numerals, spin states in a quantum register — and the curvature of the equation changes. The same physical event now demands different SGM, different ΔM, different provenance.

> **There is no base-invariant representation of physics. There is only physics as represented in a given Alphon.**
