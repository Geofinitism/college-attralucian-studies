## Abstract (verbatim)

> This essay proposes a Geofinite formulation of time as a derived quantity arising from generonic transitions within a finite symbolic system. Time is not treated as a primitive dimension, but as the ordered accumulation of compressed distinctions between successive admissible symbolic states. By situating this formulation within the historical development of temporal thought — from classical continuity to modern physical abstraction — the essay reframes time as an emergent property of finite measurement and symbolic stabilisation. The result is a shift away from continuous or absolute temporal constructs toward a model grounded in compression, uncertainty, and generonic formation.

## The Tilde: A Formal Boundary Marker

The tilde ($\sim$) is introduced here as a **formal operator on quantities**, not a stylistic choice:

| Notation | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| $T$ | Classical time — assumes continuity, infinite divisibility, pre-defined mathematical structure |
| $\sim T$ | Geofinite time — a constructed value arising from finite measurement, generonic distinction, and symbolic compression |

The tilde marks the point at which a quantity remains grounded in finite construction rather than being projected into an assumed continuum. It is a **resistance device** against symbolic lift.

**Connection to ATT_34 (The Tilde and the Basin):** ATT_34 introduced the tilde as a Geofinite distinction marker; ATT_55 operationalises it formally within a specific domain (time), demonstrating its use in distinguishing classical from Geofinite quantities.

## Generonic Distinction and the Cost of Formation

The generon is the minimal act of stable distinction within a finite symbolic system. A distinction is not free:

$$C(D) > 0$$

This **distinction cost** reflects the requirement that interaction must be transformed into a bounded symbolic form. The act of producing a stable symbol from interaction has a minimum resource requirement. In doing so, it introduces irreducible uncertainty.

The **alphonic limit**:

$$\delta_\alpha > 0$$

defines the smallest admissible distinction within the system. Below this threshold, no meaningful differentiation can be established. This is the $\sim$Time analogue of:
- $\Delta M > 0$ (FSM minimum cost of distinction, ATT_51/ATT_52)
- $\Delta x_{\min} > 0$ (kinematic resolution floor, ATT_47)
- $\delta V$ (geometric decomposition minimum, ATT_46)
- $\theta$ (truth stability threshold, ATT_48)

## ∼Time: The Formal Construction

Time emerges from the ordered accumulation of generonic compressions:

**Minimum time unit:**
$$\sim\tau_\alpha = C(D_{\min})$$

**Accumulated time over a sequence of generonic events:**
$$\sim T = \sum C(G_{i+1} - G_i)$$

Properties of $\sim T$:
- **Not continuous in its construction** — it is discrete and cumulative
- **Bounded by finite resolution** — the alphonic limit $\delta_\alpha$ constrains the minimum unit
- **Not a background** through which events pass — it is the structure that appears when distinctions are ordered
- **Emergent, not primitive** — it arises from generonic formation, not prior to it

## Classical Time as Projection

Classical time $T$ is reinterpreted as a **large-scale projection** of Geofinite time:

> Macroscopic clocks measure repeatable processes — oscillations, transitions, cycles — and project these onto a continuous axis. This projection suppresses the underlying discreteness, uncertainty, and resolution limits inherent in each generonic act.

At large scales, where distinctions are sufficiently dense and regular, continuity emerges as a **useful approximation**. The real number line is not measured; it is assumed. What appears continuous is the result of smoothing over discrete acts of distinction.

The classical apparatus is not rejected — it is relocated. It is a useful fiction (Pillar IV) built upon a more fundamental, finite process.

## The Geofinite Continuum

The Geofinite continuum is not a completed infinite structure (the real line) but a **potential**:

> It is the open capacity for symbolic distinction to be formed, stabilised, and extended under finite constraint.

It does not assert infinitely many points, completeness, or a pre-filled domain. It marks the **possibility of further distinction** — open-ended within finite constraint. Anything claiming a fully realised infinite structure belongs to the classical basin and exceeds what can be established through finite measurement.

## Symbolic Lift

ATT_55 names and diagnoses a recurring move in classical thought:

> A symbol is introduced. It is placed on a number line. The number line is treated as given. The argument proceeds as if the structure into which the symbol has been placed is itself beyond question.

This is **symbolic lift**: a finite act of measurement silently elevated into an assumed infinite structure.

The tilde ($\sim$) serves to resist this automatic lift. It marks the point at which a quantity remains grounded in finite construction rather than being projected into an assumed continuum.

**This concept applies retrospectively to the entire ATT series:** whenever a classical mathematical result appears in the essays (a limit, a probability, a commutator), the implicit move from finite measurement to assumed infinite structure is a symbolic lift. FPU (ATT_52) is the formal tool for reversing it; the tilde is the notational tool for marking where it has occurred.

## Time and Language

ATT_55 connects time to language through the ordered formation of symbols:

This aligns with ATT_04 (time as ordered compression of sequential events), ATT_01/ATT_03 (words as transducers and compressed meaning), and ATT_32 (mathematics lives inside language as linguistic compression). The Geofinite view of time and the Geofinite view of language share the same underlying structure: ordered compression of interaction.
