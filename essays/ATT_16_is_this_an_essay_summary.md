## Core trajectory

A self-referential philosophical essay that uses the act of reading as its primary demonstration. The essay traces meaning from its physical origin — gesture, pointing, sound, transduction — through lossy compression and semantic decompression, into the formal machinery of Lorenz complexity theory and Takens' delay embedding, and finally to Marina: a working small-scale language model that demonstrates the Geofinite claim that meaning is generated, not stored. The central argument is that meaning is not a Platonic abstraction but a dynamic, finite, physical measurement: the transient geometric curvature traced in the reader's phase space as they navigate the text. The essay is both a philosophical argument for Geofinitism and a live performance of its core thesis.

## Key claim

**Meaning is a dynamic measurement, not a stored entity.** Words are lossy compressors of meaning — proxies with provenance. The act of reading is a finite, uncertain, physical process of semantic decompression: comparing incoming geometric curvature against existing curvature stored in the reader's corpus. The "ghost of Plato" (the assumption that mathematical and linguistic truths inhabit a perfect, infinite, transcendent realm) is dissolved by recognising that all symbols — from pebbles to proofs — have physical provenance, measurable uncertainty, and finite geometry.

## Key evidence and arguments

- **The self-referential demonstration**: The very act of reading the essay IS the phenomenon the essay describes — the reader is making measurements, comparing against their corpus, generating transient meaning. The essay's question "Is this a philosophical essay?" is deliberately unanswered because the reader's measurement is the answer.
- **Provenance chain**: Sound → gesture → mapping (pointing) → phonetics → written text → mathematics. Every step is a transduction (lossy compression). The equals sign "=" is given as a concrete example: its history as a compression of words is forgotten, and this forgetting causes loss of meaning and apparent paradoxes in foundations of mathematics.
- **The wooden box compression**: The wooden box (high information) → wood (compressed, higher entropy, lower specific information). This is not a "game of words" but a gesture pointing to what lossy compression IS. More words are needed to recover the original meaning — demonstrating that meaning is relational, not contained in individual symbols.
- **Russell's useful fiction applied**: Bertrand Russell described a word as a useful fiction. Geofinitism operationalises this: words are lossy compressors; mathematical truths are "highly stable, shared approximations that work within the finite resolution of our measurements" — not Platonic forms.
- **Lorenz / complexity / butterfly effect**: Lorenz's discovery that computation seals the fate of equations — prediction fails because the slightest change in initial numbers cannot be predicted — demonstrates discontinuity between measurements and the measured world. Non-linear dynamical systems analysis provides the tools to measure complex systems, including language.
- **Takens applied to "Hello"**: The word "Hello" is transduced (microphone → time series → Takens delay embedding → geometric shape in phase space). The figure shows the measured topology of "Hello" — "it is no longer just a symbol; it is a topology."
- **Sentence as trajectory**: A sentence plotted in semantic phase space (Figure 2: "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog happily today before tea") shows that meaning is held not in individual tokens but in the overall shape of the trajectory.
- **Marina in operation**: Eight live outputs from the working model demonstrate Geofinite meaning generation: "Energy is what makes things happen, the capacity to perform work or cause change in the universe." — the response is a finite trajectory through a learned manifold, not retrieval.
- **Kuhnian shift**: The proposal of a Science of Philosophy (vs. Philosophy of Science) is named as a paradigm shift — new meaning that "will not easily fit into the world of existing ideas."
