## Overview

This lesson introduces the foundational question of what a word *is* within Geofinitism. It moves through five increasingly grounded models, arriving at the transducer framework: words as finite devices that compress and transform both internal corpus structure and external world measurements into symbolic form.

It is the entry point to the Attralucian Essay Series and to the broader Geofinitism claim that language is not a system of abstract references but a dynamical, measurable process.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lesson, a student should be able to:

1. Describe the five models of words and explain what each adds over the previous

## Core Concept: Transduction

A transducer converts one form of signal into another. In the Geofinitism sense:

**Internal transduction** — The word measures distances *within* the corpus. "Fire" and "smoke" are geometrically close; the word transduces this proximity into a compressed symbol that carries relational meaning.

**External transduction** — The word converts a real-world measurement into corpus space. "The voltage is 2.21V" takes a sensor reading and places it into the symbolic manifold.

Both types make words *sensors and actuators of experience* — not passive labels but active participants in the measurement loop.

---

## Connection to LLMs

Transformer attention mechanisms can be reinterpreted as performing pairwise phase-space embeddings (Haylett, 2025 — *takens-transformer* repo). This means LLM embeddings are coordinates in a geometric manifold, not static indices.

Degradation experiments (JPEG compression of embeddings) show that when precision drops, LLMs collapse into structured attractors — not random noise. This confirms the manifold geometry and supports the transducer model: the system is operating within a finite, bounded space with real attractor structure.
