## Core trajectory

Classical mathematics assumes a number N is the same regardless of which base it is written in. This essay dissolves that assumption by giving it a physical examination. Every symbolic system is a finite physical Alphon with measurable geometry; every symbol is a Nexil contained within a spherical Alphonic Limit V_α. Different Alphons produce different representational curvatures, quantified by the Spherical Symbolic Geometry Mean (SGM). The No Invariant Representation Theorem then follows directly: no bijective, volume-preserving, curvature-invariant map can exist between different Alphons. Conversion between Alphons is not translation — it is metamorphosis. Every physical law is inscribed in a specific Alphon; there is no base-invariant representation of physics.

## Key claim

The Alphonic Limit V_α = (4/3)πr_α³ is the spherical uncertainty envelope of every finite symbolic event; the SGM formula quantifies how different Alphons curve the representational manifold differently; and the No Invariant Representation Theorem proves formally that classical base invariance is geometrically impossible — **there is no bridge between Alphons, only metamorphosis**.

## Key evidence and arguments

- **Why the Alphonic Limit must be spherical**: Three constraints converge — (1) no measurable edge below the Limit; (2) no preferred orientation; (3) sphere minimises surface area for a given volume, minimising measurement noise at the boundary. The sphere is the *statistical envelope of uncertainty*, not a metaphysical ideal
- **SGM comparative table (quantum regime)**: At r_α = 0.1 nm, binary (A=2, k=40) gives SGM = 0.134 nm; hex (A=16, k=10) gives SGM = 0.110 nm; base-100 (A=100, k=6) gives SGM = 0.103 nm. All values are within one order of r_α — at the edge of measurement. At this scale, each Nexil costs ΔM ≥ 18 eV to localise. **Binary is fragile. Base-100 is robust. There is no neutral ground.**
- **No Invariant Representation proof**: Assume volume-preserving, curvature-invariant bijection f between A₁ and A₂. Then SGM_A₁(k₁) = SGM_A₂(k₂) and V_N^(1) = V_N^(2). But from the table, SGM_binary(40) = 0.134 ≠ 0.110 = SGM_hex(10). V_α is fixed by r_α — no compensation possible. Contradiction.
- **Representation is Alphonic**: Every physical law (F=ma, Maxwell's equations, Einstein's field equations) is written in a specific Alphon. "Change the Alphon and the curvature of the equation changes." There is no base-invariant representation of physics.
- **Containment, not occupation**: The Nexil is not the sphere — it is *contained by* it. The sphere is the boundary of measurability. Nexils radiate meaning equally in all directions within their containment sphere (isotropy of meaning). Distinct Nexils occupy tangent or adjacent spheres (additivity without overlap).
- **Curvature as packing density**: Larger Alphons (high A) produce denser packing of fewer Nexils → flatter curvature. Smaller Alphons (low A) require more Nexils → deeper stacking → greater curvature. Curvature is a direct measure of Alphon size.
