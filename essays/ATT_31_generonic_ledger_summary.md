## Core trajectory

The essay opens with a question asked by an accountant: what is the cost of the ink? In physics and mathematics, the analogous question has never been seriously posed. Every model, every equation, every symbol is assumed to vanish in use — to carry no weight, consume no resource, leave no trace on what it carries. This assumption is not neutral. It silently shapes the structure of models and, when extended to domains of high acceleration or cosmic distance, begins to produce systematic errors that can no longer be attributed to missing physics.

The essay refuses that disappearance. It proposes that **representation is not costless** — and that the explicit accounting of representational expenditure, through a new fifth layer called the **Generonic Cost Layer**, resolves long-standing empirical puzzles across four physical regimes simultaneously.

**Chapter 1** (The Refusal of Costless Representation) states the philosophical commitment and introduces three new concepts: the **Grand Corpus** (the total representational manifold), **basins** (stable attractor regions within it), and the five-layer ontological hierarchy, now completed with the Generonic Cost Layer as the fifth and previously missing element.

**Chapter 2** (The Grand Corpus and Its Basins) develops the full architecture. The Grand Corpus contains every symbol ever instantiated. Within it, coherent configurations self-organise into basins — attractors that persist because they can be sustained at finite cost. The Basin of Geofinitism coexists with the classical basins (Newtonian mechanics, GR, QFT, ΛCDM) without claim of exclusive truth. Classical basins produced extraordinary empirical success precisely because their local cost is negligible; they simply never kept the ledger.

**Chapter 3** (Finite Mechanics: The Empirical Engine Room) reproduces the key results of the earlier Finite Mechanics programme — three solved puzzles and two derived constants:

1. **Mercury's excess perihelion precession** (43.1 arcseconds/century): FM introduces implicit mass m_implicit = k·a; with k = 1.67 × 10²¹ kg·s²/m (calibrated by iterative bisection), the FM precession formula δφ = 2π·(k·GM_☉)/(a²(1-e²)) matches the observed excess exactly. No spacetime curvature required.

2. **Hydrogen electron stability**: classical physics predicts radiative collapse; FM stabilises the orbit via m_total = m_e + k'·a; with k' = 2.0, the orbit is fully stable; with k' = 1.65, stable but with mild precession suggestive of quantum probability distributions.

3. **Galactic flat rotation curves**: the "unseen mass" per shell M_UM,shell(r) = (v²_obs - v²_Newton)·r/G accounts for flat curves across many SPARC galaxies (R² > 0.98) with consistent power-law k'(r) profiles; no dark matter required.

4. **Fine-structure constant α**: derived from measurable quantities (μ₀, ε₀, f_Rydberg, m_e, e) without Planck's constant or c as primitives: α = e²(8f_Ryd ε₀²)^(1/3) / (2e^(4/3) m_e^(1/3)) √(μ₀/ε₀) ≈ 1/137.036.

5. **Planck's constant h**: h = (m_e e⁴/(8f_R ε₀²))^(1/3), derived from the Rydberg frequency — h and c treated as derived, not fundamental.

**Chapter 4** (The Generon as Propagating Engine) provides the ontological grounding: the FM "implicit mass" is the local manifestation of Generonic expenditure. A coherent signal or orbiting body is an unbounded Generon continuously executing; under acceleration, the Generon must allocate extra Alphonic spheres to keep the Measured Number stable. The scaling factors k and k' calibrated empirically in Chapter 3 are measurements of the **local cost density** — the rate at which the Generon expends resources under acceleration. The five-layer hierarchy is complete.

**Chapter 5** (Redshift Reinterpreted) extends the ledger to cosmological scales. A photon propagating across cosmic baselines is an unbounded Generon executing locally, accumulating cost at each step. Total accumulated cost: C(D) ≈ (D/λ)·δ. Matching to the observed Hubble law recovers:

$$z \approx \frac{H_0}{c} \cdot D = k_\alpha \cdot D, \qquad k_\alpha \approx 2.2 \times 10^{-18} \text{ m}^{-1}$$

Redshift is not geometric expansion of Platonic spacetime — it is visible Generonic cost accumulation. The Alphonic floor ε is derivable from cosmological data.

**Chapter 6** (Convergence Across Scales) presents the unified ledger: the same Generon, paying the same currency (Alphonic spheres), across all four regimes. One mechanism, four manifestations, no new ontological substances required.

**Chapters 7–8** address operational consequences for physics (calibrating λ, reformulating GR, dark components as cost gradients, Hubble tension as ledger mismatch) and close with the image of the basin: "regularity emerges not from correspondence to an unmediated absolute, but from the persistence of those configurations that can be maintained at finite cost."
